Nina's Digital Literacy Narrative

Above, you can see my Personal Learning Network (PLN) for digital literacy. It is essentially all of the resources I have available to me currently that I can turn to for help while reaching my digital literacy goals. The major points of my PLN include people I know, video resources, other digital sources, the university, and books. Some of these I actively use or have used in the past. I most often turn to video tutorials to help me with new software and I used to watch the CrashCourse playlist for computer science. I also dive headfirst into my coursework when new opportunities to develop my digital literacy arise. I was also recently exploring the IT @ UMN website and it looks like I can find great resources there, too, to learn new things. Some of the things listed in my PLN are new resources to me, such as tech-related podcasts and some of the websites I’ve listed. I'll keep exploring resources in that realm and eventually add them to my PLN. Additionally, I am open to reading books that could help develop my digital literacy. I am open to any recommendations! One of the most important aspects of my PLN is the people I know who can help me. As amazing as video tutorials are, nothing compares to having someone help you with specific questions in live time.

I’m excited to more actively explore my PLN and see where it takes me this semester and in the future.
